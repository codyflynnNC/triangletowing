5 Ways To Avoid Towing Scams

By TriangleTowing

Posted on November 8, 2016 at 9:00 PM

When a person is in need of tow truck they can be victimized by the operators if they are not aware of how to handle the situation. Triangletowing operates with the utmost respect for its customers - and we want you to be ready to deal with any company that may try to deceive, lie or outright scam you. Knowledge is power and we hope the information presented in this article will help you fend off any potential scams.

Look out for these common towing scams

  • The Body Shop Referral: Some towing companies will work with shady body shops in order to overcharge the customer and take a percentage of the sales - this is the most common scam we have seen. With that being said some referrals are completely legitimate and can actually benefit you as the customer. We ALWAYS recommend you do your own research on any business before agreeing to work with them.

  • The Bandit Tow Truck: These operators are known to take cars that are parked in private lots when the customer may only be visiting a business across the street. The particular laws vary state by state, however, in general, motorists can park in a private lot for less than 1 hour before a tow is legal. Triangletowing recommends you avoid parking in any private lots unless authorized for this reason. To make matters worse, when you are towed in this manner, the towing companies will typically will charge a large fee for each night your vehicle remains in the lot - becoming very expensive, very fast.

How to protect yourself

  • Check their credentials: Always check the documentation of the tow truck driver. Do a quick google search of the tow company if the tow truck driver appears on scene unsolicited.

  • Get it in writing: To avoid unforeseen charges, it always best to get all services the tow company plans to offer in writing with the price quoted. By doing this, you are much less likely to be scammed.

  • Keep your information private: The tow truck driver does not need your insurance information or any information about the lien holder of the vehicle. Keep this private.
